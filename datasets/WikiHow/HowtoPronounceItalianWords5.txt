@summary
impure means that the letter's sound is mixed with another sound without presence of that other.

@summary
note that for instance, in english student, letter s is impure but s in aspect is not impure.
@article
 It happens frequently at the beginning of a word. Such is when "s" followed by a consonant, the "s" could sound tz, dz or es, or even se to the listener.
 In Italian, s in both studente and asfalto is s-impura, and begins the syllable (/a-sfal-to/) as if it is a sfalto.
